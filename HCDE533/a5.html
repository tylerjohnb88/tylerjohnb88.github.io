05Meshes & Light Design

Nature Drip

I learned about some of the difficulties that come from working with 3D objects that have already been converted to .stl files. Since this is unfortunately a common way to share 3D objects in machine-based making communities, it's a good skill to build.

Tools, Materials, & Credits

  • Rhino
  • Midjourney

Process

In this project we were instructed to learn about combining and editing meshes by combining two .stl files from the internet before designing a lamp that utilizes some of what we learned. I chose a hand and a crow to perch on the hand.

After that, I planned to design a lamp based on this image I generated with Midjourney.

Generated by me, with Midjourney v6, in April, 2024

Tool Learning

Early on, I found that it's a good idea to gather as many .stl options as possible. For example, I wanted to perch a crow on a hand, but the first crow I chose had a lot of intersecting face pairs that caused some of the mesh to tear when I performed a boolean union between meshes. The faces of the triangles were so small on the crow that it didn't seem possible to repair them before the union.

It wasn't hard to find resources on how to fix holes in meshes online since this is a common problem, but I had more trouble with the resolution of the triangles being so high. After spending a good amount of time trying to fix the holes, I decided to try another crow with a resolution similar to the hand I was using. This worked much better!

Digital Design

Just like I did with the lava shapes in A2: 3D Modeling & Organic Art, I started by creating a blocky mesh structure by following this tutorial. Then, I used reference geometry to trim the top and bottom off the rounded shape.

This is where I stopped for week one and I couldn't return to it in week two due to an unexpected trip that I had to make for a family event. I would explore options for lofting between this surface and a woven set of curves to get closer to the surface texture of the final shape. Draping, Shrinkwrapping, or methods of Repelling or Attracting in Grasshopper might be good places to start.

I'm confident that this would be a design that would cast light in interesting ways. I think there are additional challenges I would have had to overcome besides the modeling of the surface.

One would have been integrating a planter into the lamp in a way that keeps water away from the lamp components. My plan for this was to either put the lamp in the upper "bulb" contour and the planter in the base, or use an LED light source that is water proof.

Another would have likely been printing since I have yet to print something this large, though I don't know what challenges I would have run into here.

One more might have been getting the lamp components to fit correctly, but I don't suspect this would have been very challenging since I'm comfortable with the accurate integration of precise components from my previous career and from A1: Lasers & Cardboard.

Integrating hardware with the lamp I made in A1.
Designing with light is really enjoyable, but challenging. I had hoped that more light would leak through the layers of leaves.
This was one of the more challenging hardware integrations I've done.

I will likely return to this project in the future because it presents several intersting challenges for mass production. The design is also something that I enjoy and want to see in reality.
