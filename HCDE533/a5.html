05Meshes & Light Design

Lava Sculpture

In week one I learned about some of the difficulties that come from working with 3D objects that have already been converted to .stl files. Since this is unfortunately a common way to share 3D objects in machine-based making communities, it's a good skill to build.

Process

In the first week of this two-week project we were instructed to learn about combining and editing meshes by combining two .stl files from the internet.

In week two I plan to continue designing a lamp based on this image I generated with Midjourney.

Tool Learning

I found that it's a good idea to gather as many .stl options as possible. For example, I wanted to perch a crow on a hand, but the first crow I chose had a lot of intersecting face pairs that caused some of the mesh to tear when I performed a boolean union between meshes. The faces of the triangles were so small on the crow that it didn't seem possible to repair them.

It wasn't hard to find resources on how to fix holes in meshes online since this is a comon problem, but I had more trouble with the resolution of the triangles being so high. After spending a good amount of time trying to fix the holes, I decided to try another crow with a resolution similar to the hand I was using. This worked much better!

Digital Design

Just like I did with the lava shapes in A2: 3D Modeling & Organic Art, I started by creating a blocky mesh structure by following this tutorial. Then, I used reference geometry to trim the top and bottom off the rounded shape.

Next, I'll try lofting between this surface and a woven set of curves to get closer to the final shape.
