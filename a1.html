A1: Blinky Lights

Here is all the documentation for A1: Blink! This was the result.

Assignment 1 was about learning how to work with resistors, LEDs, and basic circuits.

Circuit Design

A 220立 resistor was used for the red LED and 100立 resistors were used for the blue and white LEDs in order to keep the amperage below 20mA for each cicuit, the recommended max for this Arduino.

          void setup() {
            // initialize digital pins as output.
            pinMode(12, OUTPUT);
            pinMode(10, OUTPUT);
            pinMode(6, OUTPUT);
          }
          
          // the loop function runs over and over again forever
          void loop() {
            digitalWrite(12, HIGH);  // turn the LED on (HIGH is the voltage level)
            delay(1000);             // wait for a second
            digitalWrite(12, LOW);   // turn the LED off by making the voltage LOW
            delay(100);              // wait for 1/10 second
          
            digitalWrite(10, HIGH);  // turn the LED on (HIGH is the voltage level)

Homework Questions

1: Draw a graph where the X axis is time and the Y axis is voltage across the LEDs. Draw a line for each of your 3 LEDs.

2: How many LEDs could you blink at once with your Arduino? How much current would that draw?

I think the 5v limitations of the Arduino is the limiting factor. Therefore, I could chain two red, green, or yellow LEDs with a 220立 resistor for a current draw of 0.006A OR a single blue, pink, or white LED with a 100立 resistor for a current draw of 0.017A.

3: How many LEDs could you blink with your Arduino if only one were to be on at once?

4: How fast do you need to blink your LEDs until you no longer can tell that they are blinking?

Even blinking it for 1 milisecond with a second between blinks, the blink was visible. If the blink and pause between blinks is 1 milisecond, then the LED simply appears to remain on.
